ERNAKULAM

Ernakulam district is an amalgam of a hoary heritage and global growth of industry and commerce. For the outside world, Ernakulam is part of Kochi referred to in the history of Kerala as the Queen of Arabian Sea. The densely populated Ernakulam district embodies the achievements of the Kerala State in literacy, industry, trade and commerce. With an inclusive culture, relatively high per capita income and an unstoppable interest of the people in updating themselves of the political, economic, social and cultural happenings in the national and international spheres, Ernakulam represents the high modern phase of the Kerala society. The Kerala High Court, the office of the Kochi Municipal Corporation and the Cochin Stock Exchange are situated here. The Ernakulam Junction is a major railway station of the Indian Railways. Ernakulam was once the capital of the Kingdom of Cochin.

Fort Kochi is a region in the city of Kochi. This is part of a handful of water-bound regions toward the south-west of the mainland Kochi, and collectively known as Old Kochi or West Kochi. Adjacent to this is Mattancherry. The other main attraction is Marine Drive, is a picturesque promenade in the city of Kochi. It is built facing the backwaters, and is a popular hangout for the local populace.

Places to visit: Marine drive Cochin, Fort Cochin, Mattanchery, Old Harbor House, Koder House, Santa Cruz Basilica, St. Francis Church, Dutch Palace, VOC Gate, The United Club, The Bishop’s House, Fort Immanuel, The Dutch Cemetery, Bolghatty Island, Willingdon Island, Thakur House, David Hall, The Cochin Club, Cherai Beach, The Hill Palace Museum, Vamanamoorthy Temple, Kaladi, Kodanad elephant camp, Chendamangalam, Malayatoor, Bhoothathankettu, Thattekkadu Bird Sanctuary, Kuttampuzha etc…
