PALAKKAD

Palakkad, also known as Palghat, is the gateway to Kerala due to the presence of the Palakkad Gap with a width of 32 to 40 Kms in the Western Ghats and has no coastal lines. The district have many small and medium rivers, which are tributaries of the Bharathapuzha River. Its geographical position, historical background, educational status, tourism hot-spots and above all, the development activities that are carried out, are wide and varied. The district is one of the main granaries of Kerala and its economy is primarily agricultural. The district is also the land of Palmyrahs. Kalpathy rathaolsav is the main festvel of Palakkad.

Main tourist attraction is Palakkad fort in the heart of the city and Malampuzha Dam and Malampuzha gardens (15 km away). Near Malampuzha gardens, a ropeway, snake park and a beautiful aquarium. Malampuzha also has South India's first rock garden.

Silent Valley National Park, is a national park in Palakkad. The Silent Valley region is locally known as "Sairandhrivanam", which in Malayalam means Sairandhri's Forest. It is located in the Nilgiri hills, has a core area of 89.52 km2, which is surrounded by a buffer zone of 148 km2. This national park has some rare species of flora and fauna. The national park is one of the last undisturbed tracts of South Western Ghats mountain rain forests and tropical moist evergreen forest in India.
