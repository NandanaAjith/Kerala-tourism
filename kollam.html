KOLLAM

Kollam, also known by its former name Quilon, is an old seaport and city on the Laccadive Sea coast of the Indian state of Kerala. The city is on the banks of Ashtamudi Lake that took the title God's Own Country. Kollam is known for cashew processing and coir manufacturing.

Ashtamudi Lake is considered the southern gateway to the backwaters of Kerala and is a prominent tourist destination at Kollam. Jadayupaara is a tourist location in Kollam. This is a rock based tourist location with world’s largest bird sculpture, which is the primary attraction here. The sculpture is a museum as well as a multi-dimensional theater.There are activities like archery, rappelling, rock and ladder climbing etc.

Mundrothuruthu is an inland island group located at the confluence of Ashtamudi Lake and the Kallada River, in Kollam district. The island village is a tourism destination where one can see the coir retting process, coir weaving, fishing, prawns feeding, migratory bird watch, narrow canals and waterways, coconut farms on the lake shore, lagoons, mangrove plants and the beautiful tiny islands of Pathupara.

Thenmala is a tourist destination in the eastern side of Kollam district. Thenmala is the first planned eco-tourism destination in India. Boating on the lake, a rope bridge, trekking, mountaineering, biking and a musical fountain.
